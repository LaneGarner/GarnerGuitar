Beginning music theory

Music theory should not be a frightening term. Understanding the building blocks of music will help take your playing to the next level. Think of music as a language. While the best way to learn a language is through immersion, it is also helpful to study grammar. Music theory is the grammar of music. The musical alphabet contains the first seven letters of the English alphabet.

Imagine the musical alphabet as a circle from A to G returning to A after G round and round.

A-B-C-D-E-F-G-A-B-C-D-E-F-G-A-B-C-D-E-F-G ...

String names

An open string is a string plucked without fretting a note on the left-hand. The open guitar strings are named in two ways: letters and numbers. The thinnest string is the first string and the thickest is the sixth string. The letter names are notes from the musical alphabet. Use this mnemonic devide to memorize the letter name of each string:

Eddie Ate Dynamite Good Bye Eddie

Thickest string Thinnest string
6 5 4 3 2 1
Eddie Ate Dynamite Good Bye Eddie
E A D G B E
